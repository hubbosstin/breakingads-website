DOOH vs Traditional OOH: When to Use Which

Breaking Ads · 5 min read

Digital is not always better. The choice between DOOH and traditional OOH depends on your brief, your budget and your creative.

Where DOOH wins

Flexibility. You can update digital screens in real time — useful for time-sensitive campaigns like event launches, sports sponsorships or news-jacking moments. DOOH also allows dayparting: showing different creative in the morning commute versus the evening. And the production cost is lower since there's no printing.

DOOH also opens the door to dynamic creative: weather-triggered messages, live sports scores, countdown timers. When the creative benefits from data, go digital.

Where traditional OOH wins

Impact. A full-bleed 48-sheet paper poster has a physical presence that a shared digital panel cannot match. Premium traditional sites — building wraps, painted murals, station dominations — command attention in a way that stands apart from the digital noise everywhere else.

Traditional also guarantees share of voice. On a shared DOOH screen, your ad is one of several rotating. A paper site is yours, exclusively, for the duration of the booking.

The answer is usually both

The strongest campaigns combine traditional for impact and brand-building with DOOH for frequency and flexibility. The traditional site creates the moment; the DOOH network amplifies it at scale.
